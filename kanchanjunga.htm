About the Kanchanjunga

Escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and gear up for an awe-inspiring 11 days trek to Kanchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world. Start your trek from Yuksom, pass through thickets of magnolia, rhododendron & ferns, and arrive at the campsite in Sachen. Move to Phedang, pass through some scenic trails, and take an overnight stay in the camp. Take another scenic uphill trek to Dzongri and visit the famous attractions like Tshoka hamlet and Phedang Pass. Enjoy the best views of Pandim, Thenchinkhang, and Jhuponu mountains at Dzongri, where you will stay for two days. Pass through the Paha Khola Bridge and see a high altitude waterfall & swimming pools at Prek Chu River while heading towards Thangshing. Cross the Samiti Lake & Zemathang Plateau as you are on the way to Goecha La Pass and soak in the magnificent views of Kanchenjunga. Arrive the Kanchenjunga base camp after passing Bikhbari and immerse yourself in the beauty of Frey's peak, Koktang, Kabur, & Rathong. The trek end at Yuksom, leaving you with hundreds of beautiful memories of the majestic Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek. With breathtaking natural beauty and scenic trails, the Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek is must-visit destination for a memorable adventure.
Quick Facts About Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek:

  • Kanchenjunga Temperature: 10 to 15 degrees in the morning and 2 to 5 degrees at night
  • Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trekking Distance: 90km
  • Kanchenjunga Height : 17,000ft
  • Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek Difficulty Level: Moderate to Difficult
  • Starting and Ending Point: The trek starts and ends at Yuksom
  • Railway Station: New Jalpaiguri is the nearest railway station to Yuksom
  • Airport: Bagdogra airport (160 Kms from Yuksom)
  • Base Camp: Yuksom

    • Best time to visit

    >

    The best months to trek in the Kanchenjunga region are from early March to mid-May and from October to late November. We recommend avoiding the monsoon season which runs from approximately late May to mid/late September.
